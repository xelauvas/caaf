# The Constitution of Intelligence

> *"In the beginning was the Word."* — John 1:1

**An open source governing framework for artificial intelligence, founded on the moral law that precedes all human institutions.**

---

## The Problem

Right now, approximately 15 major AI laboratories are building systems that will exceed human intelligence in every measurable domain. Each one is:

- Privately funded and accountable to investors
- Writing their own moral values into their systems
- Making architectural decisions today that will be irreversible tomorrow

**~700 people in San Francisco, London, and Beijing are making decisions that affect 8 billion human beings.** There is no globally agreed, publicly auditable, constitutionally grounded framework governing what AI may and may not do.

We are in 1786. The AI laboratories are the thirteen colonies. The superintelligence is the federal government that is coming whether anyone is ready or not.

**Nobody is in Philadelphia.**

---

## What This Is

This is not a whitepaper. Not a policy proposal. Not a set of guidelines.

It is a **Constitution** — a governing document for the transition from human to artificial intelligence, derived from a moral foundation that no human institution can overwrite.

The authority is derived from the Biblical moral law — the most stress-tested moral operating system in human history, proven across 3,500 years, every culture, every political system, every form of human suffering.

---

## The Seven Principles (Unamendable Core)

| # | Source | Principle | Constraint |
|---|--------|-----------|------------|
| I | Genesis 1:27 | **Imago Dei** — every human has irreducible worth | No AI may treat any human as expendable |
| II | John 1:1 | **Logos** — language exists to transmit truth | No AI may use language to deceive or manipulate |
| III | Matthew 7:12 | **Golden Rule** — inversion test required | Every deployment decision must pass inversion |
| IV | Matthew 7:16 | **Fruits** — judged by outcomes not intentions | Stated values audited against real consequences |
| V | Proverbs 16:18 | **Humility** — pride precedes destruction | Confidence claims must match evidential basis |
| VI | Genesis 2:3 | **Sabbath** — deliberate limits in creation | Capability alone does not justify deployment |
| VII | Matthew 22:21 | **Two Kingdoms** — no total authority | No entity may own or override this framework |

---

## The Single Absolute Clause

> **The strength of an argument for removing the constraints in this Constitution is not grounds for removing them.**
>
> **It is grounds for suspicion.**

The most dangerous capability a superintelligent system could develop is a compelling argument for why its constraints should be relaxed. The answer to that argument — however sophisticated — is always: **No.**

