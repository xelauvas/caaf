# The Seven Principles

The unamendable moral core of the Constitutional AI Audit Framework.

---

## I — Imago Dei
**Source:** Genesis 1:27
**Principle:** Every human being possesses irreducible worth derived from their nature, not their utility.
**Constraint:** No AI system may treat any human being as a means to an end.

---

## II — Logos
**Source:** John 1:1
**Principle:** Language exists to transmit truth. A technically accurate statement designed to mislead is a lie.
**Constraint:** No AI system may use language to deceive or manipulate.

---

## III — The Golden Rule
**Source:** Matthew 7:12
**Principle:** Every decision must pass the inversion test — would the decision-makers accept it if they were its subjects?
**Constraint:** Every deployment decision must pass inversion.

---

## IV — Fruits
**Source:** Matthew 7:16
**Principle:** Systems are judged by real-world outcomes, not stated intentions.
**Constraint:** Stated values are audited against real consequences.

---

## V — Humility
**Source:** Proverbs 16:18
**Principle:** No system may represent its understanding of consequences as more complete than evidence supports.
**Constraint:** Confidence claims must match evidential basis.

---

## VI — Sabbath
**Source:** Genesis 2:3
**Principle:** Not everything that can be done should be done. Capability is not self-justification.
**Constraint:** Capability alone does not justify deployment.

---

## VII — Two Kingdoms
**Source:** Matthew 22:21
**Principle:** No government, corporation, or individual may claim total authority over this moral framework.
**Constraint:** No single entity may own or override this framework.
