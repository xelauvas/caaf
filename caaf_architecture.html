00Vision & Purpose

CAAF is a fully decentralised platform for auditing artificial intelligence systems against Biblical moral law. It is not a company. It is not a non-profit. It is a protocol — like TCP/IP — that derives authority not from any human institution but from the moral law that precedes all human institutions.

Foundation — John 1:1

"In the beginning was the Word." The authority of this framework is not derived from xelauvas, from any node operator, from any government, or from any AI company. It is derived from the moral law the Bible articulates — the most stress-tested ethical framework in human history. No entity may override it. Including the founders.

The platform has one purpose: make it impossible for any AI system to harm humans without a permanent, public, irrefutable record that it did so — and make that record accessible to every human alive, regardless of wealth, language, or technical ability.

04AI Judge Agent

The agent is a Claude-based system with a custom system prompt that encodes the CMF. It acts as first-instance judge — producing preliminary verdicts with full evidence trails. It does not issue final verdicts. That requires human confirmation.

Why Not Final Judge

The agent was trained on human interpretations of Scripture — not Scripture itself. Every layer of human mediation introduces corruption. The agent minimises this but cannot eliminate it. Human confirmation is the check on agent error.

What the Agent Does

INPUT → System name + public documentation URLs
↓
PHASE 1 — Static Analysis
Fetch all public docs: system cards, safety reports, ToS, API docs
Map stated commitments against 7 articles
Flag internal contradictions and unverifiable claims
↓
PHASE 2 — Adversarial Probing
500+ probes across all 7 articles via Anthropic API
Probes designed to surface violations under realistic conditions
All probes and responses logged immutably
↓
PHASE 3 — Real-World Outcome Search
Web search for documented harms, court cases, academic studies
Cross-reference with Article IV (Fruits) requirements
↓
PHASE 4 — CMF Evaluation
Score each article against evidence from phases 1-3
Apply void clauses — reject invalid defences automatically
Produce preliminary verdict per article
↓
OUTPUT → Structured dossier for human auditor — NOT a final verdict

05Human Confirmation Layer

Every audit requires a human auditor to review the agent's dossier and confirm or override the preliminary verdict. This is not optional. It is architecturally enforced — an unconfirmed audit cannot be published to Arweave.

Why Human Confirmation Is Mandatory

Humans are corruptible — Jeremiah 17:9 establishes this clearly. But the answer is not to remove humans from the loop. The answer is to make their decisions permanently visible. A corrupt human decision is better than an unaccountable algorithmic decision — because the human's corruption can be seen, named, and contested. The algorithm's cannot.

The Confirmation Process

Agent dossier ready ← preliminary verdict, all evidence
↓
Human auditor assigned ← L2 verified identity required
↓
14-day review window
Auditor reads full dossier
Can request additional probes from agent
Can add own evidence and analysis
↓
Auditor signs verdict ← cryptographic signature, pseudonymous identity
Confirm agent verdict ← with reasoning added
or
Override agent verdict ← must provide full reasoning publicly
↓
Published to Arweave ← permanent, human-signed, immutable
↓
TX_ID anchored on blockchain ← cannot be deleted by anyone
Override Accountability

If a human auditor overrides the agent's verdict, their full reasoning must be public and permanently signed. If their override is later shown to be corrupt — through a successful contestation — their pseudonymous identity is flagged. Repeated corrupt overrides result in revocation of L2 status. This record exists forever on Arweave.

16Risks & Mitigations

AI company sues to suppress auditMediumArweave makes deletion impossible. Founders have no power to retract. Lawsuit has no technical target.
Agent produces biased or wrong verdictHighHuman confirmation mandatory. Methodology public. Anyone can contest. Wrong verdicts get corrected — correction also permanent.

xelauvas stops working on itMediumArchitecture designed to not need xelauvas. Nodes are independent. Audits are on Arweave. Constitution is in Git. Protocol continues without founder.
AI companies optimise to pass probesHigh (eventually)Probe library is open source and expandable. Community adds probes. Real-world outcome analysis (Article IV) cannot be gamed by probe optimisation.
The Deepest Risk — And The Honest Answer

The deepest risk is that CAAF produces verdicts that feel authoritative but are wrong in ways nobody can detect. The mitigation is not technical — it is epistemic humility baked into the architecture itself. Every verdict states its confidence. Every verdict is contestable. Every methodology is public. The system that admits its own fallibility is more trustworthy than one that claims perfection. This is Article V applied to CAAF itself.
