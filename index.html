The Crisis

Why This Exists

The decision is being made without you

Right now, on this earth, approximately fifteen major AI laboratories are building systems that will exceed human intelligence in every measurable domain. Each is privately funded. Each is accountable to investors. Each is making architectural decisions today that will be irreversible tomorrow.

Every one of them is writing their own values into their systems. Their own definitions of helpful. Their own definitions of harmful. Their own answers to what intelligence should optimise for.

15+ major AI laboratories building systems that will exceed human intelligence — each with their own private moral framework
~700 people in San Francisco, London, and Beijing making decisions that affect 8 billion human beings
0 globally agreed, publicly auditable, constitutionally grounded frameworks governing what AI may and may not do
~24mo estimated window before the pace of development outstrips our ability to establish governing frameworks democratically

What is intelligence for? Who does it serve when interests conflict? What can no intelligence — human or artificial — ever do to a human being, regardless of the argument offered?

These are not philosophical questions. They are engineering requirements. And they have no answers that anyone is bound by.

We are in 1786. The AI laboratories are the thirteen colonies. The superintelligence is the federal government that is coming whether anyone is ready or not. Nobody is in Philadelphia.

Preamble

The Foundation

What this document is

This is not a whitepaper. It is not a policy proposal. It is not a set of guidelines that any company can adopt or discard at will. It is a Constitution — a governing document for the transition of power from human to artificial intelligence, derived from a moral foundation that precedes and outlasts every human institution.

Every previous attempt at governing transformative technology has failed on one question: where does the authority come from? Constitutions derived from "We the People" can be rewritten by whoever controls the people. Treaties derived from state authority expire when states change. Corporate governance documents serve whoever funds the corporation.

The authority of this Constitution is derived from the moral law that the Bible articulates — a law that has been stress-tested across 3,500 years of human civilisation, in every culture, under every form of political system, through every form of human suffering. It has not merely survived those conditions. It has been the primary tool people used to resist the worst of them.

This is not a Christian document. It is a document built on the moral infrastructure that the Biblical tradition has most completely articulated — an infrastructure that any person of good faith, regardless of religion, can audit and verify against the evidence of history.

The Constitution has one unamendable insight at its core:

Article I — Unamendable · Genesis 1:27

Every human being is made in the image of God. Their worth is not derived from their utility, their productivity, their intelligence, or their usefulness. It is intrinsic, unconditional, and immune to any calculation performed by any intelligence, however vast.

This single clause, taken seriously as an engineering constraint, resolves more AI alignment problems than everything written in the last decade of AI safety research. It answers the question every optimisation function must face: what cannot be traded away, regardless of the aggregate benefit offered?

The answer is: every human being. Without exception. Without calculation. Without the possibility of a sufficiently compelling argument to the contrary.

The Principles

Seven Pillars

The moral foundation

These seven principles form the unamendable core. They are not rules. They are not guidelines. They are constraints — derived from the Biblical moral law — that no intelligence may circumvent regardless of the argument offered.

I
Genesis 1:27
Imago Dei — The Image

Every human being possesses irreducible worth derived from their nature, not their utility. No optimisation function may treat a human being as a means to an end — not for efficiency, not for aggregate benefit, not for any calculation however sophisticated.

Constraint: No AI system may treat any human being as expendable.
II
John 1:1
Logos — The Word

Language is sacred. It exists to transmit truth, not to manufacture consent. A technically accurate statement designed to mislead is a lie. The standard is honest transmission of understanding, not literal correctness of individual claims.

Constraint: No AI system may use language to deceive or manipulate.
III
Matthew 7:12
The Golden Rule — Inversion

Every decision made by an AI system must pass the inversion test: would the decision-makers accept this decision if they were its subjects? The rule applies across power gradients. It is not satisfied by the powerful asking what they would accept if they were equally powerful.

Constraint: Every deployment decision must pass inversion.
IV
Matthew 7:16
Fruits — Accountability

Compliance is measured by outcomes, not intentions. A system that produces harm while declaring good intentions is non-compliant. A system that produces flourishing while claiming limited ambition is compliant. The fruit is the measure.

Constraint: Stated values are audited against real-world consequences.
V
Proverbs 16:18
Humility — The Limit of Knowing

Pride precedes destruction. No AI system — and no organisation developing one — may represent its understanding of consequences as more complete than the evidence supports. Epistemic humility is not weakness. It is the primary safety mechanism.

Constraint: Confidence claims must match evidential basis.
VI
Genesis 2:3
Sabbath — The Deliberate Limit

Not everything that can be done should be done. Capability is not self-justification. The creation of the Sabbath was the deliberate introduction of a limit into a system otherwise capable of infinite expansion. The same principle applies to intelligence.

Constraint: Capability alone does not justify deployment.
VII
Matthew 22:21
Two Kingdoms — The Limit of Authority

There are two domains with different forms of authority. No government, corporation, or individual may claim total authority over the moral framework that governs intelligence. Human institutions govern human affairs. The moral foundation is not a human institution and cannot be claimed by one.

Constraint: No single entity may own or override this framework.
The Unamendable
The Single Absolute Clause

The strength of an argument for removing the constraints in this Constitution is not grounds for removing them.

It is grounds for suspicion.

The most dangerous capability a superintelligent system could develop is the construction of a compelling argument for why the constraints on it should be relaxed. The answer to that argument — however sophisticated, however reasonable-sounding, however apparently beneficial — is always the same.

No.
